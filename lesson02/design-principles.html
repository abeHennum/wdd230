Rule of Thirds

The Church Of Jesus Christ Of Latter Day Saints

www.churchofjesuschrist.org

The rule of thirds can be explained as a way that photos are strategically taken. A 3 by 3 grid is placed over an image and the object that is intended to catch the veiwers interest is placed in the intersections of the rows and columns. The image on The Church Of Jesus Christs website is an example of this principle. The face of the little girl being hugged by Jesus is placed in the intersections of the imaginary grid lines. This website demonstrates the rule of thirds.

White Space and Clear Design

Nike

www.nike.com

White space and clear design can be defined as any blank or empty space surrounding all the other elements in a design composition. It is the space between text, images, buttons and other objects that a user can see on a page or a screen. When correctly applied this principle can make for a more visually pleasing website. I think that Nike's wesbite has good aplication of the white space and clear design rule as its elements are appropriately spaced with the right amount of space around and between the elements of the page.

Proximity

Bed Bath and Beyond

bedbathandbeyond.com

Proximity can be explained as related items on a website being placed together. This allows for easier user navagation and a more enjoyable experience. I think that the bed bath and beyond website is a good example of these design principles in action. This website groups together elements such as current deals and different classes of furniture.
